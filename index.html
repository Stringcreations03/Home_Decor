Step 1: Design Mapping

Converting design structures mathematically into high-density coordinates.

Step 2: Base Selection

Selecting premium warp-resistant MDF or sustainable natural pine wood sheets.

Step 3: Surface Treatment

Executing precision sanding followed by matte obsidian prime coat applications.

Step 4: Grid Transfer

Securing vector alignments onto wood boards to guide nail insertion maps.

Step 5: Nail Calibration

Individually hammering nails to matching depth parameters.

Step 6: Fiber Formulation

Choosing cotton, metallic wires, or silk strings to match aesthetic guidelines.

Step 7: Structural Anchoring

Securing the base starter knot onto anchor nails.

Step 8: Geometric Weaving

Interlacing threads across coordinates over long artisan sessions.

Step 9: Layer Splitting

Individually overlaying dark and light layers to finalize shadow densities.

Step 10: Tension Realignment

Tightening loose strands and concealing final structural knots.

Step 11: Surface Restoration

Vacuuming lint and applying finishing polishes onto wood margins.

Step 12: Protective Framing

Encasing the board with premium custom wooden configurations.

Step 13: Mounting Setup

Affixing heavy-duty steel mounting fixtures and hanging wires.

Step 14: Certificate Registry

Validating thread tightness and pasting the signed Certificate of Authenticity seal.

Step 15: Museum Packing

Wrapping in protective paper layers and shipping in secure, shockproof boxing.
